# Vertical Hierarchy Diagram Template

Generate a vertical diagram showing levels, layers, progression, or hierarchy from top to bottom (or bottom to top).

## Layout Structure

- **Orientation**: Vertical stacking of 3-6 levels
- **Direction**: Can be top-to-bottom (descending) or bottom-to-top (ascending/pyramid)
- **Levels**: Distinct horizontal bands or shapes for each level
- **Connectors**: Arrows or visual flow between levels

## Visual Specifications

### Pyramid/Triangle Style (ascending)
- Shape: Stacked horizontal sections that widen toward bottom
- Top: Smallest section (peak)
- Bottom: Widest section (base)
- Each level: Different shade from the green palette
- Gradient: Deep Forest (#1D442F) at top â†’ Soft Mint (#BAD8C4) at bottom

### Stacked Bands Style
- Shape: Equal or varying height horizontal bands
- Full width or 80% width with rounded ends
- Colors: Alternating brand greens or progressive gradient
- Separator: 4-8px gap or thin line between bands

### Level Labels
- Position: Centered within each level
- Primary text: Level name/title
- Secondary text: Description or metrics
- Font: Playfair Display (titles), Figtree (descriptions)

### Connectors (if needed)
- Style: Arrows or chevrons between levels
- Direction: Indicating flow (up or down)
- Color: Dark Brown (#403422) or Gold (#D5A972)

### Side Labels (optional)
- Position: Left or right of main diagram
- Content: Numbers, percentages, or annotations
- Style: Figtree, 12-14px, aligned with corresponding level

### Background
- Solid Warm Cream (#E6E7DD)
- Main diagram centered with generous margins

## Typography
- Level titles: Playfair Display or Figtree Bold, 18-24px
- Level descriptions: Figtree Regular, 12-16px
- Side labels: Figtree Medium, 12-14px
- Axis labels (if any): Figtree, 10-12px

## Proportions
- Image aspect ratio: 3:4 or 4:5 (portrait) OR 4:3 (landscape for wide hierarchy)
- Level heights: Equal or proportional to importance
- Side margin: 15-20% on each side for labels
- Gap between levels: 8-16px

## Critical Requirements
- Clear visual hierarchy from top to bottom (or reverse)
- Each level distinct but connected
- Text legible at all levels
- Progressive color scheme that makes sense
- Balanced composition
- No logo placement
