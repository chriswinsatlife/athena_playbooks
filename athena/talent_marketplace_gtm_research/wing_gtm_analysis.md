## Sales-Led vs. Product-Led Growth Mix 
Wing Assistant exhibits a blend of **sales-led and product-led growth** elements, tailored to the nature of its service. On one hand, Wing is *sales-assisted* ‚Äì every client is typically matched with a dedicated Customer Success Manager (CSM) and there‚Äôs a human touch in onboarding. For higher-value prospects or larger teams, Wing employs a sales-led approach where potential clients can *‚ÄúBook a Meeting‚Äù* or demo call with a sales consultant to discuss their needs. This sales-led component ensures that bigger clients get a high-touch experience, custom quotes, and any security/contractual assurances needed. It also aligns with Wing‚Äôs managed-service model ‚Äì the salesperson and CSM essentially shepherd the client from signup through successful integration of the assistant into their workflow.

On the other hand, Wing incorporates **product-led growth (PLG)** tactics to minimize friction for smaller customers. Notably, Wing offers a *‚ÄúTry Free for 7 Days‚Äù* trial for new clients ([wingassistant.com](https://wingassistant.com/developer-talent-v3/?_cio_id=ccb30601d0a001818b03&id=%40milledmail.com#:~:text=We%20will%20find%20the%20Perfect,Software%20Developer%20for%20Your%20Project)). This free trial is a PLG move: it lets prospects experience having a Wing assistant for a week at no cost, which often convinces them of the value before they ever talk to a salesperson. The self-service ‚ÄúGet Started‚Äù flow on Wing‚Äôs website allows a customer to pick a plan and sign up in a few clicks. Once signed up, the Wing Workspace app (the product) takes over to guide onboarding. Wing‚Äôs platform is quite robust ‚Äì it includes communication tools (chat, calls, Slack integration), task management (kanban boards, calendars), file sharing, and even an AI-powered quality monitoring system ([www.finsmes.com](https://www.finsmes.com/2022/03/wing-assistant-raises-2-1m-in-seed-funding.html#:~:text=,Password%20management)). By providing this software platform, Wing creates a product-like experience where clients and assistants collaborate. Many routine aspects (assigning tasks, tracking progress) are handled through the app, reducing the need for continual sales or support intervention. This ‚Äúproductization‚Äù of a service is a key growth enabler; it allows Wing to serve more clients at scale because much of the interaction is streamlined via the app.

The **mix** of sales-led and product-led is well-calibrated. Initially, growth was more product-led with viral referral from happy customers (typical of a concierge app pivot). As Wing moved upmarket slightly (serving SMEs and even departments of larger firms), they layered in a sales approach for high-touch deals. They still keep the funnel open for self-serve signups, but ensure every client ‚Äì even self-serve ones ‚Äì gets a human CSM for success. This strategy has paid off in retention: Wing reports an industry-leading average client tenure (reportedly *‚Äúthe highest in the industry‚Äù* at well over two years) ([wingassistant.com](https://wingassistant.com/web-developer-v2/?_cio_id=ccb30601d0a001818b03&id=%40milledmail.com#:~:text=%2B%20Months)). That suggests the combination of an easy-to-use product and supportive account management keeps customers sticking around. In summary, Wing‚Äôs growth model starts with product-led hooks (free trial, quick onboarding in 24-48 hours, user-friendly app) to get clients in the door, and then uses sales/customer success to expand and retain those clients (cross-selling additional assistant services, ensuring satisfaction). It‚Äôs not purely self-serve viral growth, but it‚Äôs highly efficient compared to a traditional enterprise-only sales model.
