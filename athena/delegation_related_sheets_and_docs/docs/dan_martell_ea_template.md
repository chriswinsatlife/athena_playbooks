# **North Star Principles** {#north-star-principles}

## **Protect Time** {#protect-time}

Filter all inbound requests to keep an eye out for those people/places/things that are important and help prioritize them. 

## **Efficiency in Calendar** {#efficiency-in-calendar}

Be as efficient as possible. Be clear, committed, and include context.

## **Ensure that Everyone Appreciates the Response** {#ensure-that-everyone-appreciates-the-response}

Whether that’s a clear ‘yes’ with the appropriate calendar info for an upcoming meeting, or a ‘not at this time’ reply, every email and correspondence should be answered clearly and kindly.

## **Design the Play** {#design-the-play}

Be preemptive. Come prepared and aware of all the moving pieces and how to address them ahead of time.

# **Task Cadence** {#task-cadence}

## **Daily** {#daily}

This is the daily flow for EA tasks.

1. [Review \[Name's\] Calendar](#preferences) for the day ahead  
2. [Process the \[Name's\] Inbox](#processing-the-inbox)  
   1. Goal: 0 Inbox by 10 AM EST and again at 5 PM EST  
3. Process your Inbox   
   1. Schedule any calls or meetings requested (outside of clients)  
   2. Sure up any speaking engagements  
4. Create a to-do list for the day, complete by EOD.   
   1. Add to it any ordering  
   2. Voice note requests  
   3. Slack message request  
   4. Processing emails that require specific actions (Like signing legal documents, or customer service requests)  
5. Prepare for meeting with \[Name\]  
   1. Update your [Sync Meeting Agenda](#building-the-meeting-agenda)  
6. Research/ book any requested travel  
7. Briefly review the \[Name's\] Calendar for 6-weeks ahead  
8. EOD, before signing off:   
   1. Go through your inbox and address all lingering questions,   
   2. Write summaries or notes to emails you’ll work on tomorrow to denote any progress you’ve made,   
   3. Compile your ‘To Do’ list for the following day.

# **Client-EA Sync Meeting** {#client-ea-sync-meeting}

Syncing regularly will help us become clear on expectations and outcomes. First, you will meet with \[Name\] every day. After 4-6 weeks, you’ll meet three times a week. After three months, you will meet once a week. 

## **Building the Meeting Agenda** {#building-the-meeting-agenda}

Building and maintaining an up-to-date meeting agenda is a vital activity each day. Every meeting agenda should include these 8 parts, in this order:

1. A Title. Outlined like so, “Sync \- \[EA name\] & \[Client Name\], \[date\]”   
2. Meeting Action Items. List the names of all meetings \[Name\] has had since the last time you spoke. Confirm there is nothing that you need to do for those.   
3. Calendar review. Have the calendar pulled up and open so that \[Name\] can easily follow along.   
4. \[Name's\] Agenda. Ask \[Name\] if he has any feedback, or anything on his list that he’d like to discuss with you.   
5. Closing the Loop. List any action items that have been resolved or finalized since you’ve last met.  
6. Assistant to Review. Present any challenging emails or opportunities that \[Name\] might be interested in.   
7. Projects. List in order of importance all the projects you are working on that need assistance from \[Name\] to resolve. Be succinct and offer 3 resolution options for any problem.  
8. Questions for \[Name\]. Create a list of questions to help you understand the business and \[Name\] better. If you have extra time during your meeting, run through as many as possible. 

## 

## **Running the EA Meeting** {#running-the-ea-meeting}

1. Come prepared. Have all necessary documents, links or items that need to be reviewed, opened and easily accessible.   
2. Begin by sharing your screen so that \[Name\] can follow along to the meeting agenda and calendar review.  
3. Stay succinct, summarize all points as quickly and clearly as possible.  
4. Take notes of any action items that need to be taken care of after the meeting.   
5. Ask questions. This is your time to get to know \[Name\] and ask as many questions as you have.
